Beluga Whales

Characteristics:

Weight: between 1-1.5 tons

Size: between 12-20 feet

Food: fish, crustaceans, and worms.

Biological Name: Delphinapteris leucas The average lifespan of a wild Beluga whale is between 30-50 years, whereas in captivity they very often die before they reach 30. Beluga whales are very social animals who congregate in groups known as ‘pods’. These whales can either seperate into different pods once they are matured but majority stay within the same pod their whole life. Each pod has their own unique set of noises; clicks, whistles, and clangs that are specific to only members of the pod. Within captivity they are forced into artificial ‘pods’ or social groups which can lead to negative effects on their emotion and physical behaviour.
